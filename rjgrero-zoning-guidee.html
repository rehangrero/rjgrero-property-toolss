Key Zoning Risk Factors
Assessment Framework
Risk Factor What to Assess Red Flags Impact on Score
Rezoning Risk
 Check current zoning status against the UDA Development Plan. Verify no pending zoning amendments in the gazette. Proximity to industrial zones, coastal buffer zones, or government-reserved land. High — can collapse asset value overnight
Floor Area Ratio
 Verify permitted FAR and plot coverage. Understand what can be built or extended on the land. FAR inconsistency with existing structure. Unapproved extensions or additions. Medium — affects development potential
Land Use Conformity
 Confirm current use of asset matches permitted use under zoning classification. Residential property operating as commercial. Unapproved change of use. High — creates legal and compliance exposure
Coastal Zone Rules
 Coastal Conservation Act — 300m buffer from high water mark. Verify CCD approvals if near coast. Any structure within 100m of coast without Coast Conservation Department approval. Critical — demolition orders possible
Easements & Rights of Way
 Survey plan review. Check for utility easements, road widening proposals, government reservations. Road development plans that cut through or reduce the plot. Utility corridors. High — permanent loss of usable area
Land Reform Restrictions
 Ceiling on Housing Property Law and Land Reform Commission restrictions on agricultural land. Agricultural land exceeding permitted ceilings. Land Reform Commission encumbrances. Critical — title may be unenforceable
Building Line Setbacks
 Road Development Authority setbacks on national roads. Local authority setback requirements. Structures built closer than permitted setback lines. Road widening buffers. Medium — affects future buildability
Foreign Ownership Rules
 Land (Restrictions on Alienation) Act No. 38 of 2014. Foreigners cannot hold freehold land. Leasehold (99yr) or condominium units only. Freehold land offered to foreign buyers. Unclear ownership structures. Critical — acquisition may be void
Environmental Clearance
 Central Environmental Authority approvals for developments over prescribed thresholds. Development without Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) or EIA where required. High — can halt development or force demolition

Regulatory Authorities
Key Bodies to Engage
Urban Development Authority
UDA
Primary urban zoning and development control authority. Issues development permits, approves building plans, and maintains the official zoning maps for all urban areas.
Zoning Maps Dev Permits FAR
Survey Department
Survey Dept
Issues official survey plans, cadastral maps, and topographic maps. Essential for boundary verification and land measurement accuracy.
Survey Plans Cadastral Maps Boundaries
Land Reform Commission
LRC
Administers the Land Reform Law. Clearance required for agricultural land transactions to confirm absence of ceiling restrictions and vesting orders.
Ag Land Ceiling Rules Clearances
Coast Conservation Dept
CCD
Regulates all development within the coastal zone under the Coast Conservation Act. Mandatory approval for any construction within 300m of the high water mark.
Coastal Buffer Coastal Permits 300m Rule
National Physical Planning Dept
NPPD
Prepares national and regional physical plans. Key for checking road widening proposals, government acquisition intentions, and infrastructure development plans.
Road Widening Acquisitions National Plans
Central Environmental Authority
CEA
Environmental clearance body. IEE and EIA approvals required for prescribed development projects. Critical for industrial, hotel, and large-scale residential projects.
EIA IEE Env Clearance
Municipal / Urban Councils
CMC / UC
Local authority building permits and approvals. Colombo Municipal Council is primary for Colombo city. Verify local development plan classification independently of UDA.
Local Permits Building Plans Local Zoning

Tourism Dev Authority
Sri Lanka Tourism Development Authority. Regulates tourism zone developments. Foreign investment in tourism projects requires SLTDA approval and registration.

Advisory Notes
RJ Grero Framework
Best Practice
Always obtain zoning confirmation in writing from the UDA or relevant local authority before signing any sale and purchase agreement. Verbal assurances from agents or sellers carry no legal weight and are a primary source of post-acquisition disputes in Sri Lanka.
Diaspora Buyer Note
Foreign nationals and Sri Lankan diaspora with overseas citizenship are subject to the Land (Restrictions on Alienation) Act 2014. Freehold land ownership is restricted. The primary compliant structures are: (a) 99-year leasehold, (b) condominium unit ownership under the Apartment Ownership Law, or (c) ownership through a qualifying Sri Lankan-incorporated company.
High Risk Scenario
Agricultural land offered as developable without explicit UDA or LUPPD conversion approval is one of the highest-risk acquisition scenarios in Sri Lanka. Conversion approval is a process — not a guarantee — and should be treated as incomplete until gazette confirmation is in hand.
